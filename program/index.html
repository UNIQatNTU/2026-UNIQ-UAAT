2026 UNIQ Resonance Workshop

The UNIQ (University Alliance of NTU, UIUC, and KU) will host the 2026 UNIQ Resonance Workshop, from June 16 to 18, 2026, at National Taiwan University in Taipei. This workshop is a pivotal event designed to foster collaboration and synergy among researchers from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), National Taiwan University (NTU), and Kyushu University (KU). This workshop is strategically designed for two primary groups within our academic community: recipients of the 2025 seed funding program and faculty members interested in applying for the 2026 seed funding program.

The theme of this year’s workshop, “Transition to Better Synergies for Stronger Success,” focuses on moving away from individual research silos toward high-impact, multi-institutional alliances. By participating, you will gain the tools to harmonize your expertise with premier university partners and the strategic power of programs like UAAT and SPARK Taiwan.

The workshop will feature a blend of keynote speeches, panel discussions, and interactive sessions, all aimed at equipping attendees with the knowledge and resources needed to build successful collaborations. We encourage all interested faculty members to attend, especially those who are considering applying for the 2026 seed funding program, as this workshop will provide valuable insights and networking opportunities to enhance your chances of success in the application process. We look forward to seeing you there!

The workshop consists of three days program. The first day will be dedicated to keynote speeches and panel discussions on building stronger synergies for better success, with sessions on how to build successful collaborations and leverage the resources of UAAT and SPARK Taiwan. The second day will be dedicated to using AI and machine learning for advanced researches and higher education. The third day will be reserved for interactive sessions and networking opportunities, where attendees can connect with potential collaborators and discuss their research ideas in a more informal setting.

The workshop is free to attend, but registration is required. Please see the registration page for more details.

Organizers and Sponsors

This workshop is organized by the University Alliance of NTU, UIUC, and KU (UAAT), with support from the Office of Research and Development at National Taiwan University.
